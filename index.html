A DRIP

The smooth dotted ribbon drifting upwards and separating into a zillion tiny pleas for mercy—why did you have to do this, why did it have to be you, shouldn't you have been better—is made of phantoms and remorse and bitterness and a fire spell you had no right to learn; magic that you never bothered to control. His fingers burn as he twists the tiny flicker of flame closer to his skin, pulling it back when it gets too close and the feeling is almost something close to freedom.

Alice sits watching him, quietly. Sev knows there's something she wants to say, can see it in the way the edges of her eyes seem to harden. Once upon a time, she'd bombard him without a second thought. Once upon a time, they also hid things like crushes. So maybe what they have is better, or they messed up somewhere down the line and that's why they're stuck wondering when the other shoe is going to drop every time they are alone together.

Maybe it's for the best.

"You know, you can talk to us if you need to, right? Me and Zane."

She says it so gently. Carefully. Sev looks at her, where she is, stares her in the eye and takes in this familiar setting of the waves laping on the shore and he...

He feels like he's been left adrift.

There's nothing.

"Of course!" he grins and it slides on so easily that the weight of it feels like home. "You guys are my best friends! Why wouldn't I?"

Alice smiles, warm and small and so full of everything and he grins back like she's the sun. Sometimes Sev wonders if she has a subconscious penchant for danger, with how she's stuck with him for so long. And the thought rings empty through him, like something's been carved out of him, and he's slowly starting to realize that feeling isn't coming back.

He watches as she drops herself off from her seat, twisting beside him while latching and winding her hand within his own. She pulls, leading him forward and he lets her, grin dropping and fitting into a slot that's more like a smile, soft and gentle and reserved for those that are the center of his universe. The way she lights up, laughing and turning and leading him forward lights something in his mind that tells him this is right, but something inside him twists and he can't—

It feels like there's something missing in the back of his eyes.

A DROP

"I need you," he says, like a prayer, and Mikaela contemplates lying his body down right then and there and offering his entire being as sacrifice.

He doubts it would be enough—gods, he knew nothing about him would ever be enough—but maybe the thought alone, the intent, would be enough that he could repay even a fraction of the debt that must be budding for every second Alice stays around him. He wants to sob into her shoulder and beat his fists into her chrst, tear her heart out and kiss her breath away and he hates the way he's so fucked up now, or maybe he hates that he's always been this way.

Either way, he hates. Hates and hates how it's the only feeling aside from love devotion that doesn't feel like zero.

"Alice, you can't just say things like that so easily—"

"And what about this makes you think it's easy?!"

Alice's correction is sharp, quick, and it's welts over Mika's stilled heart that thrash about like they're trying to jolt it back to life. It's enough to make his hands twitch, nearly drawing away as green eyes stare and stare and stare, stare and see too much, far too much, but Mika can't turn away. He's transfixed, always has been and always will be, and that is something that can be said with ease.

Only because he's broken. Too dependent. Reaching for the wrong things. Wanting to be burned by the light, the sun; he doesn't care if he falls and falls and burns

"Because you're you," he says quietly, almost without waver, but compensation comes in the form of Mika's fingers digging grooves into Alice's shoulder. "You care too hard and give too easily. Even in a world like this...Especially in this hell, you continue to give and care. For so many—"

His voice breaks, Mikaela's breath hitching in his throat as his eyes burn, because that's the truth, wasn't it? The absolute truth. Alice's world had grown so far beyond Mika, and was ever expanding while Mika's own remained closed. Stagnating. Rotting and breaking, maybe, but resolute. He was a chapel that had been burned to the ground, but he painted his ash upon the one person who was left to remember what he was.

A remnant.

"I need you."

Alice repeats herself and Mika can't tell if he wants to laugh or cry.
